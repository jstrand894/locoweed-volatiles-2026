Locoweed VOC Profile Analysis: Endophyte Effect
Oxytropis sericea — Greenhouse & Field
Generated: 2026-03-17 (updated)
============================================================

Overview
--------
We tested whether the presence of the endophytic fungus Undifilum oxytropis
(SWA-positive / E+) is associated with differences in whole-plant volatile
organic compound (VOC) profiles in Oxytropis sericea. Plants were spatially
paired (one E+, one E-) to control for microsite and genetic variation.
Analyses used centered log-ratio (CLR) transformation of raw VOC emission
rates (ng/g/hr), with Euclidean distance on CLR values (= Aitchison distance)
as the dissimilarity metric.

METHODOLOGICAL NOTES:
  1. Year was removed from the PERMANOVA formula in both datasets. Year is
     fully collinear with the stratification variable (strata = year), making
     it inestimable as a separate term (Df = 0). Year effects are instead
     controlled for via the permutation strata. Year-stratified models were
     run separately to confirm consistency of results across years.
  2. SIMPER was run on raw ng/g/hr values (Bray-Curtis dissimilarity) rather
     than CLR-transformed data. CLR values can be negative, which renders
     Bray-Curtis undefined. An Aitchison-consistent compound ranking was also
     computed from squared mean CLR differences between groups.
  3. The following compounds were excluded from the greenhouse analysis as
     non-biogenic or likely contaminants: tetradecane and variants, cyclohexene,
     o+C5xe, homosalate, ethylhexylsalicylate, diethylphthalate (synthetic UV
     filters / plasticizers), benzothiazole (industrial rubber additive),
     butylatedhydroxytoluene / BHT (synthetic antioxidant; SPME contaminant),
     dodecylacrylate (synthetic acrylate ester), hexadecane, tridecane,
     pentadecane, nanodecane (straight-chain alkane contaminants), p-trimethyl
     (likely trimethylbenzene; anthropogenic aromatic), caprylyl acetate (not
     established as biogenic in Oxytropis), and dimethylnona (identity
     ambiguous; excluded for conservative compound selection). Retained
     compounds are all established biogenic plant volatiles (terpenoids, green
     leaf volatiles, fatty acid-derived aldehydes, methylsalicylate).
  4. The field PERMANOVA includes collection.type (flowers vs. leaves) as a
     term, but this variable is nearly perfectly confounded with pair2: only
     1 of 49 pair2 groups contains both tissue types. As a result,
     collection.type is undetectable as a marginal term after accounting for
     pair2. Tissue-stratified models (flowers only; leaves only) were run
     as the appropriate test of the endophyte effect within each tissue.

OVERALL SUMMARY
============================================================

Across both greenhouse and field datasets, endophyte status (E+ vs. E-)
was not associated with detectable differences in VOC emission profiles.
Combined PERMANOVA tests were non-significant for swa in both datasets
(GH: F = 0.64, R² = 0.003, p = 0.798; Field: F = 1.53, R² = 0.009,
p = 0.171). Year-stratified models confirmed that this null result is
consistent across both years in both settings. Tissue-stratified field
models likewise showed no endophyte effect (flowers: F = 0.83, p = 0.523;
leaves: F = 0.97, p = 0.472). No individual compounds were identified as
indicators of endophyte presence by IndVal.

Plant pair identity was the dominant source of variance in both analyses
(GH: R² = 0.726, p = 0.003; Field: R² = 0.584, p = 0.001), indicating that
plant-level genetic or microsite factors drive VOC composition far more than
endophyte infection status.

Within-group dispersion did not differ between E+ and E- plants in either
dataset (GH: p = 0.801; Field: p = 0.744), supporting the validity of the
PERMANOVA results for the swa term.

Compound-level analyses (SIMPER on raw data; CLR ranking) identify
caryophyllene as the largest single contributor to GH group separation in
CLR space (21.3% of squared Aitchison centroid distance, higher in E-),
followed by hexenolacetate (12.9%), bocimene (11.1%), and z3hex (10.4%).
In the field, heptanal and bocimene together account for ~67% of the CLR
centroid distance. In all cases these percentages describe the composition
of a small overall separation — the between-group effect size is low across
both datasets and analytical approaches.
